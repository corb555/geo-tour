Catastrophic Geomorphology

While tectonic forces built the foundation of the Northwest, the surface was radically sculpted by the erosional power of Continental Glaciation.

Towards the end of the Pleistocene, the Cordilleran Ice Sheet formed a dam blocking the Clark Fork River, creating Glacial Lake Missoula.

When the ice dam failed, it released a megaflood with a discharge of 20 million cubic meters per second. This high-energy flow stripped the topsoil and created distinct channels (coulees) into the underlying basalt bedrock.
