Pull-Apart Basin

Death Valley represents the extreme of extensional tectonics. It sits at the interface between the rigid, granitic batholith of the Sierra Nevada and the fracturing crust of the Great Basin.

The valley is a structural graben. While the surface is 282 feet below sea level, data suggests the bedrock floor lies beneath 8,000 feet of alluvial fill, creating a tectonic depression nearly two miles deep.

This rapid vertical displacement creates immense relief. Telescope Peak rises 11,000 feet directly from the basin floor, evidence of active, high-angle normal faulting.
