Extreme Extension

At Death Valley, the stretching of the continent reaches its violent limit. You are looking at the gap opening up between the solid, rigid block of the Sierra Nevada (West) and the fractured, pulling-apart crust of the Great Basin (East).

The valley floor has dropped to 282 feet below sea level. But that is just the surface; the actual bedrock floor is buried under 8,000 feet of gravel. If you removed that fill, it would be a tectonic hole nearly two miles deep.

High above, Telescope Peak rises 11,000 feet. This vertical drop—one of the greatest on Earth—is active proof that the crust here is still ripping open.
