Hotspot Track

Why is there a 400-mile wide flat valley in the middle of the Rocky Mountains?

50 million years ago, a continuous wall of mountains stood here. Then, the North American plate drifted over a stationary Mantle Plume (Hotspot).

It didn't melt the mountains; it obliterated them. A chain of super-volcanoes—Owyhee-Humbolt, Bruneau-Jarbidge, Twin Falls, and the massive Heise field—detonated one by one as the plate moved over the heat source.

The Heise eruption was 2,500 times larger than Mount St. Helens, instantly turning mountains into dust and collapsing the crust.
