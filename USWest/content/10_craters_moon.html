Hotspot Track

The Snake River Plain is an anomaly, a 400 mile wide flat depression in the middle of the rugged Rocky Mountains.

This plain marks the path of the North American Plate over a Mantle Plume (a connection geologists only realized in the mid-1970s). Prior to 16 Ma, continuous mountain ranges stood here. As the plate drifted southwest, a sequence of Caldera-forming super-eruptions effectively obliterated the high relief, collapsing the crust into the low-lying plain visible today.

The Heise Volcanic Field (6.6 Ma) produced rhyolitic eruptions 2,500 times larger than Mount St. Helens, burying the ancient topography under thousands of feet of ignimbrite.
