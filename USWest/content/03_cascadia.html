Subduction Zone

Off the coast of the Pacific Northwest lies a violent Convergent Margin. Here, the dense, oceanic Juan de Fuca Plate is being driven beneath the North American continent.

This massive collision produces three dramatic consequences. First, it scrapes layers of marine rock off the diving plate, thrusting them upward to build the Coastal Mountain Ranges. Second, the plates lock together, building immense strain that eventually releases in catastrophic Megathrust Earthquakes (Magnitude 9.0+), such as the great Cascadia earthquake of 1700.
