Subduction Zone

Off the coast of the Pacific Northwest lies a Convergent Margin. Here, the denser, oceanic Juan de Fuca Plate is colliding with and subducting beneath the less dense North American continent.

This process creates an Accretionary Wedge of scraped-off sediment offshore and generates immense strain onshore. This locked interface is capable of generating Megathrust Earthquakes (Magnitude 9.0+), similar to the 2011 Tohoku earthquake in Japan.
