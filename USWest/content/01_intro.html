Tectonic Regimes

The Western United States offers one of the world's most dramatic displays of Plate Tectonics. The landscape is being actively deformed by the interaction of three distinct tectonic regimes:

  • Transform Motion: To the south, the Pacific Plate is shearing laterally past North America along the San Andreas Fault, locking due to friction before snapping in massive strike-slip earthquakes.
  • Convergent Subduction: To the north, the Juan de Fuca Plate is diving beneath the continent. This collision fuels the stratovolcanoes of the Cascadia Volcanic Arc and primes the coast for megathrust earthquakes.
  • Continental Extension: In the interior, the crust is pulling apart. This stretching fractures the continent into hundreds of tilting fault blocks that form the parallel mountains and valleys of the Great Basin.
