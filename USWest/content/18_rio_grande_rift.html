Continental Rift

In the Albuquerque Basin, the continent is actively tearing itself open. This is the Rio Grande Rift.

To the west lies the rigid Colorado Plateau; to the east, the stable Great Plains. The Rift is the violent seam where these two massive blocks are pulling apart. This is a rare geologic eventâ€”one of the few places on Earth where a continental plate is actively splitting.

As the land stretches and the valley floor drops, the Earth's crust thins. This allows the hot mantle to bulge upward, creating a heat anomaly beneath the state that fuels hot springs and volcanoes. If you removed the sediment beneath Albuquerque, you would look down into a fracture nearly 20,000 feet deep.
