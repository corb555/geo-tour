Continental Rifting

The Rio Grande Rift is a rare example of active continental rifting. Unlike the broad extension of the Basin and Range, this is a narrow, deep fracture separating the Colorado Plateau from the stable Great Plains Craton.

As the lithosphere stretches and thins, Asthenospheric Upwelling brings hot mantle rock closer to the surface. This creates high heat flow anomalies throughout the region, powering geothermal systems and volcanism.

The rift basin itself has dropped dramatically; geophysical evidence indicates the sediment fill in the Albuquerque Basin is nearly 20,000 feet thick.
