Transform Plate Boundary

The line slicing through the mountains is the boundary where two massive tectonic plates—the North American and the Pacific plate—grind past one another.

This is a Transform Fault. Unlike plates that crash or pull apart, these plates slide sideways. The land on the left (West) is moving north relative to the land on the right.

But they do not slide smoothly. The plates are currently locked by friction, bending the crust like a spring. When the stress becomes too great, the rock snaps, creating a major earthquake.
