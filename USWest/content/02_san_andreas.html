Transform Boundary

The San Andreas fault is a classic example of a Right-Lateral Strike-Slip Fault. It represents the transform boundary where the Pacific Plate moves northwest relative to the North American Plate.

Motion here is governed by Elastic Rebound Theory. The plates are not sliding smoothly; they are locked by friction. As tectonic forces continue to push, the crust deforms elasticallyâ€”storing potential energy.

When the stress exceeds the shear strength of the rock, the fault ruptures. The stored energy is instantly released as seismic waves, resulting in major earthquakes.
