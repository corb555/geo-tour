Large Igneous Province

During the Miocene epoch (approx. 16 Ma), the crust here fractured, unleashing a massive Flood Basalt event. This is widely believed to be the initial arrival of the Yellowstone Hotspot plume head.

In sharp contrast to the high-viscosity eruptions of the Cascades, this was **low-silica (Mafic)** magma with **low viscosity**. It erupted from fissures in massive volumes, creating fluid sheets that flowed like water across the landscape. The resulting stack of lava is over a mile thick, weighing down the crust to create a distinct subsidence basin.
