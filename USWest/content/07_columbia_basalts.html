Large Igneous Province

During the Miocene epoch (approx. 17 Ma), the crust here fractured, unleashing a Flood Basalt event. This is widely believed to be the initial arrival of the Yellowstone Hotspot plume head.

Unlike the high-viscosity eruptions of the Cascades, this was low-viscosity tholeiitic basalt. It erupted from fissure vents in massive volumes, creating sheet flows that inundated the topography. The resulting stack of lava flows is over a mile thick, weighing down the crust and creating a distinct subsidence basin.
