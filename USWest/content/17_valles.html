Resurgent Caldera

The Valles Caldera,is a volcanic depression so vast that its rim forms the entire horizon. While it appears today as a peaceful, grassy bowl, its circular form reveals its violent past as a collapsed "supervolcano."

Surprisingly, this caldera is not unique. It is merely the youngest of a sequence of calderas in the Jemez Mountains. They exist here due to the intersection of the Rio Grande Rift and the Jemez Lineament. This structure thinned the crust, allowing magma to punch through repeatedly, stacking one massive volcano on top of another for millions of years.
