Jemez Lineament

The Jemez Lineament traces a 1.7 billion-year-old Proterozoic Suture Zoneâ€”a deep scar formed when an ancient island arc collided with North America, trapping water-rich oceanic crust within the mantle.

Today, this ancient weakness intersects the active extension of the Rio Grande Rift. This structural convergence triggers a combination of decompression melting from rifting and flux melting from the trapped water, creating a primary locus for explosive volcanism.

  • Valles Caldera: A resurgent caldera complex formed by the collapse of the magma chamber roof following the eruption of the Bandelier Tuff (1.25 Ma).
  • Mount Taylor: A composite stratovolcano.
  • El Malpais: A field of quaternary basaltic flows and lava tubes.
