Continental Glaciation

Lava and floods aren't the only sculptors here. During the last Ice Age, the Cordilleran Ice Sheet—a wall of ice over 3,000 feet thick—moved south from Canada.

The ice acted like a belt sander, gouging out the deep north-south troughs that are now Puget Sound and Lake Washington. The hills of Seattle are actually piles of Glacial Till—debris left behind by the retreating ice.
