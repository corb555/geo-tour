Supervolcano System

The hotspot currently resides beneath the Yellowstone Plateau. Unlike the fluid lava that formed the Columbia River Basalts, the magma here is Silicic (Rhyolitic). This high silica content makes the magma highly viscous, trapping gas until it detonates.

This explosive violence created the park's defining geological feature: the Yellowstone Caldera. When the massive Lava Creek eruption (640 ka) emptied the magma chamber, the roof collapsed, leaving a crater 45 miles wide.

Within this crater, the shallow heat source powers the world's highest concentration of geysers, while Resurgent Domes rise and fall as the magma chamber recharges below.
