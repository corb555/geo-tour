Supervolcano System

The hotspot currently resides beneath the Yellowstone Plateau. Unlike the fluid, low-silica lava of the Columbia River Basalts, the magma here is **High-Silica (Rhyolitic)**.

This high silica content makes the magma **extremely viscous**, trapping massive amounts of gas pressure until it explodes. This violence created the park's defining feature: the **Yellowstone Caldera**. When the massive Lava Creek eruption (640 ka) emptied the magma chamber, the roof collapsed, leaving a crater 45 miles wide.

Within this crater, the shallow heat source powers the world's highest concentration of geysers, while Resurgent Domes rise and fall as the magma chamber recharges below.
