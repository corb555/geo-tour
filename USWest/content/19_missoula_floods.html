Catastrophic Geomorphology

While deep tectonic forces built the structural foundation of the West, the surface topography was radically reworked by a much younger force: The Ice Age.

Towards the end of the Pleistocene (approx. 15,000 to 13,000 years ago), the massive Cordilleran Ice Sheet descended from Canada. A lobe of this ice formed a dam blocking the Clark Fork River, creating Glacial Lake Missoula.

When the dam failed, it unleashed a megaflood with a discharge of 20 million cubic meters per second. This high-energy flow stripped the topsoil and carved distinct channels (coulees) directly into the volcanic bedrock.
