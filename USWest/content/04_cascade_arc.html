Continental Volcanic Arc

The High Cascades—from Mount Shasta to Mount Rainier—are classic Stratovolcanoes. Their existence is tied to the subduction of the Juan de Fuca plate which begins roughly 100 miles to the west.

This is fueled by Flux Melting. As the oceanic plate descends into the mantle, heat and pressure drive volatiles (water and CO2) out of the slab. These fluids lower the melting point of the overlying mantle wedge, generating magma that ascends through the crust to form a Volcanic Arc.
