Volcanic Arc

The red marks —from Mount Shasta to Mount Rainier and further—are Stratovolcanoes. They are the exhaust vents for the subduction occurring off the coast.

As the subducting oceanic plate dives deep underground, heat and pressure drive water out of the rock. This water melts the rock above it, creating magma that rises to the surface to form a chain of explosive volcanoes known as a Volcanic Arc.
