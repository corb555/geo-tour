Continental Volcanic Arc

The third consequence of subduction occurs inland. As the oceanic slab plunges 60 miles into the mantle, it triggers the formation of the Cascade Volcanic Arc.

Intense heat and pressure drive volatiles (water and CO2) out of the sinking rock. This generates magma with intermediate silica content and high viscosity. This thick magma piles up to construct steep, explosive Stratovolcanoes like Mount Rainier and Mount St. Helens.
