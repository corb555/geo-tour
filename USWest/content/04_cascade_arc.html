Continental Volcanic Arc

The third consequence of subduction occurs inland. As the oceanic slab plunges 60 miles into the mantle, it triggers the formation of the Cascade Volcanic Arc.

Intense heat and pressure drive volatiles (water and CO2) out of the sinking rock. These fluids lower the melting point of the overlying mantle (Flux Melting), generating magma that ascends through the crust to construct massive Stratovolcanoes like Mount Rainier and Mount St. Helens.
